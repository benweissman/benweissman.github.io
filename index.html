Ben Weissman

Chicago-based human   ·   Teacher, adviser, researcher, and musician  ·   Captivated by many hobbies

I'm a doctoral candidate in the musicology PhD program at Northwestern University with several years combined experience writing, editing, teaching, advising, researching, and performing administrative duties in nonprofit organizations. I love editing, advising, and helping students and organizations develop their ideas.

My dissertation studies practitioners of musical experimentalism in 1970s California, their pursuit of musical novelty in the Cold War era, their earnest integration of New Age thought into the study of music, and the influence of philanthropic funding organizations on their music. I have researched topics including early synthesizer film music and the history of recording technology, and I have presented research about sound poetry (Society for American Music) and early sound film of Russian art song (Music and the Moving Image). While at Northwestern, I have designed and taught four undergraduate music history courses. I currently work as a graduate assistant at the Office of Fellowships at Northwestern.
